    001 — About

    The Process

    Rotoscoping is the technique of tracing over footage, frame by frame, to produce realistic action in animation. Originally patented in 1917 by Max Fleischer, it remains one of the most painstaking and rewarding crafts in animation.

    This project documents a full semester of study — 12 weeks of practice, experimentation, and iteration. From basic line tracing on Week 1 to complex multi-layer compositing by Week 12.

    Tools used include Adobe After Effects, Procreate, DaVinci Resolve, and custom Python scripts for frame extraction.

    • Frame-by-frame hand tracing
    • Photoshop & After Effects compositing
    • Motion blur simulation
    • Colour & tone matching
    • 3D reference integration
