    The Course

    Multimedia and Authoring (MMA) is a studio-based course exploring the intersection of traditional animation, digital 3D production, and motion design. Students develop skills across software, craft, and creative research.

    The course is structured around a semester-long project: each student selects a short film sequence and rotoscopes it — tracing movement and form frame by frame — while simultaneously building a library of 3D assets that inform and enrich the animation work.

    The connection between 3D modelling and rotoscoping is intentional. Blender renders of the solar system serve as lighting references, background environments, and structural guides for the traced animation layers.

    By Week 12, each student has produced a 60-second rotoscoped sequence — 5 seconds contributed per week — combining hand-traced frames with 3D-rendered environments and professional sound design.
