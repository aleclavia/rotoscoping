    Multimedia Authoring & Animation

    Where traditional animation technique meets cutting-edge 3D technology — exploring rotoscoping as the bridge between live-action reference and animated expression.

    🎓

    The MMA Course

    The Multimedia Authoring (MMA) program bridges creative disciplines — blending motion design, 3D modeling, compositing, and traditional animation into a unified practice.

    Students engage with both historical techniques and modern pipelines, using industry tools like Blender, After Effects, and Procreate to bring concepts to life.

    ✏️

    What is Rotoscoping?

    Rotoscoping is the frame-by-frame tracing of live-action footage to produce realistic, fluid animation. First patented by Max Fleischer in 1917, it remains one of the most nuanced crafts in animation.

    By studying real movement through rotoscoping, animators develop a deep understanding of weight, timing, and motion that elevates all their work.

    🪐

    The 3D Connection

    3D rendering in Blender runs alongside rotoscoping in this course. The solar system project challenges students to master lighting, texturing, and shading — skills that directly inform rotoscoped compositing.

    Rendered planets serve as dynamic backgrounds and lighting references for the rotoscoped sequences.

    📽️

    The 60-Second Film

    Over 12 weeks, each student builds a 60-second rotoscoped animation — 5 seconds of new animation produced each week. The cumulative project is the capstone of the semester.

    Weekly progress is documented here, showing the evolution from first frame to final composite — a living record of craft in progress.

    Semester Arc

    WK 01–03
    Foundations. Introduction to rotoscoping, workspace setup, frame extraction, and basic line tracing over still and moving reference.
    WK 04–06
    Form & Light. Line quality studies, colour passes, shadow mapping, and introduction to 3D solar system modeling in Blender.
    WK 07–09
    Hybrid Work. Compositing rotoscoped figures over 3D renders. Motion blur, smear frames, and atmospheric FX layers.
    WK 10–12
    Final Composite. Sound sync, multi-layer compositing, 4K export, and final review. The complete 60-second film is assembled and submitted.
